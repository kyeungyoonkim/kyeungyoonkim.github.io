Data Visualization & Accessibility (Draft)

In progress · Working notes on designing charts and dashboards that more people can actually read and use.

What this draft is about

This page collects in-progress notes as I systematize what I’ve been learning about accessible data visualization: how color, typography, layout, and alternative text affect whether people can understand a chart—especially screen reader users and people with low vision or color vision deficiencies.

Early focus areas

  • Color choices that work for common forms of colorblindness and low contrast environments.
  • Clear hierarchy in titles, subtitles, axis labels, and annotations so readers know what to look at first.
  • Writing alt text and figure descriptions for charts so non-visual users still get the main takeaway.
  • Reducing cognitive load by simplifying encodings, legends, and the number of simultaneous comparisons.

Status

This is a living draft. I’ll continue to refine it as I document concrete examples from my own dashboards and course projects.
