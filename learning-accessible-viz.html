Clear Data Visualization for Insurance Dashboards

In progress · Working notes on designing risk and insurance dashboards that stakeholders can review quickly and accurately.

What this draft is about

This page collects in-progress notes as I systematize what I’ve been learning about clear dashboard design for risk and insurance settings: how color, typography, layout, labels, and alternative text affect whether people can understand a chart, compare segments, and trust the takeaway.

Early focus areas

  • Color choices that make loss trends, claim counts, exposure changes, or segment differences easy to compare.
  • Clear hierarchy in titles, subtitles, axis labels, and annotations so readers know what risk question the chart answers.
  • Alt text and figure descriptions that preserve the main takeaway for exported reports and review packets.
  • Reducing cognitive load by simplifying encodings, legends, and the number of simultaneous comparisons.

Status

This is a living draft. I’ll continue to refine it as I document concrete examples from my own dashboards and course projects.
