About Yoon Kim

I am passionate about leveraging data science, artificial intelligence, and human-computer interaction (HCI) to create inclusive and accessible technologies. My research interests lie at the intersection of machine learning, assistive technology, and ethical AI, with a strong focus on improving accessibility for underserved communities.

I hold a BS degree in Applied Statistics from Purdue University (December 2024), where I also earned an Applications in Data Science Undergraduate Certificate. I am currently applying my statistical and data analysis expertise as a Manufacturing Quality Engineer at Qcells in Cartersville, GA, since March 2025.

My ultimate career goal is to conduct cutting-edge research at the doctoral level, focusing on the intersection of data science, AI, and HCI to drive innovation in accessible technology and healthcare solutions.

My technical expertise encompasses a robust foundation in statistical modeling, machine learning, and AI applications. I am proficient in programming languages like Python, R, and SQL, alongside data visualization tools such as Tableau and PowerBI. I leverage these skills to extract meaningful insights from complex datasets and develop impactful data-driven solutions. For a comprehensive list of my technical skills, please refer to my Skills page.

Outside of my professional pursuits, I enjoy traveling and watching MLB games, which offer creative outlets and broaden my perspective.
