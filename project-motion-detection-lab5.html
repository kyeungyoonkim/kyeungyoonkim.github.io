Motion Detection & Localization

November 2023 · Course laboratory — Embedded camera pipeline and classical vision for near–real-time motion highlighting.

Summary

A group laboratory assignment spanning breadboard hardware bring-up, streaming small grayscale frames from a microcontroller to a host computer, and applying background subtraction with a simple spatial rule to indicate where movement appears in the field of view. Implementation details and artifacts are not shared publicly out of respect for course and team policies.

Scope (high level)

  • Hardware: Raspberry Pi Pico with a compact camera module, wired on a breadboard per course specifications.
  • Acquisition: Serial link from the board to a PC; fixed-size grayscale frames assembled for display and processing.
  • Vision: Adaptive background modeling to separate static scene content from foreground motion, then a grid-based heuristic to flag regions of interest and draw simple on-screen cues.

Takeaway

The lab underscored how fragile embedded I/O can be (connectivity, timing, and shared access to the port) and how lightweight, interpretable rules can still be useful for motion alerting in constrained environments.
